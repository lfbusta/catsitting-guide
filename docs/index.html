House Rules

  • Please don't smoke inside.
  • Feel free to invite friends over. The walls are super thick so you probably won't bother the neighbours. As long as nothing is damaged and things are cleaned we are perfectly happy.
  • Make sure the cats always have access to both litter boxes i.e. don't leave the door to the boathroom closed. Its ok to throw Mochi in the bedroom while you're cooking/eating though. Just let her out once youre done.

Doors and Keys

  • The building, apartment, and trash room doors all unlock with the same key.
  • Our front door closes by itself so make sure you always have the key with you when you go out.
  • We have two spare keys in case things go wrong: A neighbour in the same building, and a friend who lives nearby. We'll give you their contact details over whatsapp.

Activity and leisure equipment

  • We'll share the wifi with you over whatsapp.
  • We have an apple TV with Netflix and Disney plus. You'll have to use the Apple TV remote to control it. It's a terrible remote, sry.

Bed Room

  • We will leave empty spaces for you in the wardrobe (right door).
  • Make sure the window is closed at all times to avoid the cats getting out. You can leave it open at the top for ventilation if you want to.

Heating

  • The apartment stays at about 19ºC with the heating off in the winter so we usually don’t turn on the heating. But if you want you can turn it on by rotating the dial next to the front door and in the bedroom next to the door.
  • We have underfloor heating so it might take up to 8 hours for the apartment to heat up, so there is no point switching it off at night.

Cleaning

  • We recommend washing the sheets and duvet cover at least every other week. If you wash them in the morning they should be dry by the time you go to sleep at night.
  • Please use the squeegee (hanging in the shower) to wipe water off the glass panes after showering. Berlin has very hard water so calcium deposits build up fast if you don’t do this.
  • If you you see that water in the shower is not draining well, please clean the filter of the shower drain. You might have to do this every 2-3 weeks.
  • Please vacuum the apartment because it gets very dusty with cat fur quite quickly. You can use the vacuum cleaner (next to the washing machine) or the Roomba (on the washing machine). When using the roomba, please make sure to empty it and remove hair and dust from the moving parts. The roomba cannot handle the rugs, so you'll need the vacuum cleaner for that.
  • There's a feather duster and a rug to dust surfaces on the white kallax on top of the washing machine.
  • Cleaning supplies for the kitchen are found in the kitchen cupboard under the sink.
  • Cleaning Supplies for the bathroom and toilet are found in the bathroom cupboard.
  • Dishwasher soap, and other kitchen cleaning supplies can be found in the cupboard under the sink.
  • Washing machine soap is kept in a container on top of the washing machine.

Trash and Recycling

  • Garbage bags are all kept in the cupboard under the sink.
  • The trash cans in the apartment are color coded: Red takes plastic and composite packaging, and grey is for other rubbish. There is a paper bin lined with a tote bag we use for paper trash, and a special bio bag for food waste (it’s green- we’ll leave one out for you). The cat litter goes in the green bin next to the washing machine. It’s generally good to double bag this as the litter gets quite heavy.
  • Garbage can be taken out to the main apartment building garbage room, which is accessible through the basement. The directions are: - Go to -1 floor with the elevator - Exit the elevator and go to the car park (door on your left) - Walk straight from the car park entrance to the other side of the room. On your left enter the door labeled “Bossestrasse 6a” - Go up the stairs to the ground floor - Exit to the inner garden of the apartment block - Walk right for a few steps. There will be a door on your right which you need your key to enter. The rubbish bins are also color coded, and you can check the pictures they have to see what goes where. Yellow is for plastics and packaging, black is for general rubbish, brown for food waste, and blue is generally for paper. Cat litter goes in the black bin.

The Cats

Our cats are two sisters from the same litter from 2018.

Mochi

Playful, SMART, and food obsessed. She WILL get into any food you leave accessible to her. Even if its inside your bag. Even food left in plastic bags is not safe. We recommend to put Mochi in the bedroom when you’re cooking or even eating. Please don’t let her eat any of your food either, we are trying to train her to not expect food from people. Also, be careful when she gets very playful and starts rolling around on the floor. If you pet her she can play very rough and bites hard. Just disengage and play with her using a toy instead. Despite her quirks, she’s a very loving cat and loves cuddles and attention. She likes to get pets on your lap, and when she’s in a good mood she’ll often go to the kitchen and start rolling around the floor. This means she’s happy and wants to get a bit of attention! Proceede with caution.

Maki

Cuddly, calm, and gentle.

Feeding Instructions

They eat wet food twice a day (once when we have lunch and once before we go to sleep) and dry snacks once a day (when we have dinner).

Maki eats 70g of wet food per meal.

Similar to Mochi, except it’s 60g per wet food meal and she eats in the living room. Please also feed her 15g dry food in between but use the food puzzle board instead of the ball for Maki. Distribute the food around the board and place it on the floor.

Maki can take a bit to warm up to people, but she is very affectionate and loves attention when she gets to know you. She prefers to be the one approaching you, not the other way round :). She’s really playful as well and is much better behaved than Mochi when it comes to food! But she is quite fussy with the litter box. If it’s not clean she might not use it!

Mochi eats 80g of wet food per meal.

70g of wet food from the can per meal, and two meals per day, feeding once at 12:00 and once at 23:00, times can be adjusted. Please also add a bit of warm water to the bowl, about 30ml per meal. We don’t have a separate water bowl so we give them their water through wet food and extra water. Mochi normally eats in the living room next to the front door. In between the wet food meals please also give 15g of dry food with the rolling ball food puzzle. Mochi likes this one, all you have to do is fill up the ball and place it on the floor, she will push it around to get the food out. It’s not so important when you give the dry food, as long as it’s in between the wet food meals. It’s also OK to let Mochi use this food puzzle in the living room. Food is found in the cupboard above the freezer in the kitchen. You can use the cylindrical Tupperware to store leftover wet food in the can. There may already be some stored in this Tupperware in the fridge. The dry food is in a large bag on the same shelf as the wet food cans. We will place the food puzzles, the weighing scale and their bowls on the kitchen counter so it’s easy for you to find.

Exercise and Play

Cat toys are found on top of the shelf on top of the TV. Both cats love the wand toy with a worm on the end. There are extra worms in the blue bag. Please play with them whenever you can but don’t leave the toys out without watching them because they will try to eat them.

Toilet and Grooming

Both cats use the litter tray in the bathroom and the tray in the living room, both need to be cleaned twice per day, ideally right before they eat. It’s very important to get everything out, even the stuff that’s stuck on the side! We use clumping litter, and throw the clumps into the green lided bin near the washing machine which we take out when full. It’s really important to keep on top of the box cleaning because otherwise Maki might pee somewhere else! She is very sensitive to a dirty box.

Not a fancy place, but is still our favorite traditional sushi place in Berlin! Go on Wednesdays or Saturdays for Happy Hour deals.

Hako Ramen

Delicious ramen with amazing hot plum wine.

Japanese food with a great selection. Their Yakisoba is delicious! (Don’t go for ramen here though. Hako is better for that!).

Delicious asian inspired vegan food. Great ambiance and good prices too. Its a must if you're after some filling vegan food to share with friends.

Dalbhat

It’s far but very worth the trip. Traditional Nepali cuisine. Better to book in advance.

Brammibals

A bakery with fantastic vegan doughnuts.
